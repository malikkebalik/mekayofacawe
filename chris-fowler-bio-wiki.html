Who is Chris Fowler?

Chris Fowler is an American sports broadcaster known for serving as a reporter for ESPN. Moreover, Chris came into the spotlight after starting to appear on College GameDay.

In addition, he has also covered the College Football Playoff semifinal games and the College Football National Championship.

Chris Fowler- Bio, Early Life, Parents, Age, Ethnicity

Chris Fowler was born on August 23, 1962, in Denver, Colorado, United States. He grew up with his family in his hometown in Rockford, Illinois, and at State College, Pennsylvania.

Furthermore, his father Knox Fowler was a theater professor.

Education

Concerning his education, Chris attended General William J. Palmer High School, in Colorado, the United States in 1980.

Later in 1985, he completed his graduated with a Bachelor of Science from the University of Colorado.

Chris Fowler- Professional Career

Chris Fowler began his career as an intern in the sports department of KMGH-TV.

After that, the sports anchor worked for a couple of years at KCNC-TV as a production assistant, a producer/writer, and a sports reporter.

In July 1986, Chris Fowler moved to ESPN as the host of Scholastic Sports America.

In addition, he began to host the College GameDay football roadshow in 1990.

Moreover, he also hosted College Basketball until 2006.

Apart from that, Chris Fowler has served as a broadcaster in ESPN’s Summer X Games from 1995 to 2000, Winter X Games from 1998 to 2000, horse racing, and much more.

Besides this, the veteran anchor is also the head studio anchor for tennis tournaments broadcast on ESPN, such as Wimbledon, Australian Open, French Open, and, US Open.

In 2010, Chris Fowler covered the 2010 FIFA World Cup alongside Mike Tirico. Furthermore, he is also serving as the host of the ESPN Classic show SportsCentury.

Currently, his annual salary records at $1.5 million. Furthermore, he has a net worth of $4 million US.
