As previously stated, David Hefner is one of the Playboy founder’s four children. On August 30, 1955, he was born to his father, Hugh Marston Hefner, and mother, Mildred (“Millie”) Williams. He is, in reality, the magazine publisher’s and his first wife Millie’s first son.

Hefner grew up in one of the wealthiest families and led a lavish lifestyle. Nonetheless, despite his father’s celebrity, David has kept himself mostly out of the spotlight.

David Hefner Has Three Brothers and Sisters

He is not the family’s only child. Christie Hefner, Cooper Hefner, and Marston Hefner are his three other siblings. Cooper and Marston are his stepbrothers, while Christie is his biological sister.

His step-brothers are the result of his father’s second marriage to Kimberley Conrad. All of his siblings are well-established and successful in their own fields. Cooper, his younger brother, is the chief creative officer of Playboy Enterprise. He is also the founder and CEO of the new media firm Hop.

Similarly, Marston is affiliated with Playboy magazine and is an actor. He has appeared in a number of TV shows and documentaries, including Hef’s Runaway Bride, America’s Book of Secrets, and others. Unfortunately, he was arrested and imprisoned for a year in 2012 for domestic abuse against his girlfriend Claire Sinclair.

Christie, his biological sister, is a businesswoman and activist.

Christie Hefner, David Hefner’s biological sister, is a wealthy businesswoman. She has been with Playboy magazine for almost 30 years and is very active in the family company. She was also the CEO of Playboy for approximately 20 years.

She allegedly started working for the magazine in 1975 and was named president of Playboy Enterprises in 1982, and then CEO in 1988.

She is also a philanthropist, having donated $30 million to CORE Chicago. With all of her charity and commercial initiatives, she has been named one of Forbes’ 100 Most Powerful Women several times.

What is David Hefner’s occupation? His College Education and Career

David, unlike his sister and step-brothers, is not involved in the family business. Rather, the star child has forged his own way and established his own company. He enrolled in the University of California, Santa Cruz in 1999 and graduated in 2003 with a bachelor of arts in film and digital media.

Hugh Hefner’s son David began working as a freelance assistant editor/editor in Los Angeles, California in October 2005, following his graduation. He eventually worked as an editor for Associated Television’s discussion shows. David has also worked on a number of feature picture projects, including Beyond, Robosapien Rebooted, and Shut Up and Shoot.

Hefner began working as a writer and director for Smoking Cat Films in Los Angeles in 2007. David created and directed his first feature film, ‘Forgotten Pills,’ while working at Smoking Cat. It also took home the main jury prize for best feature at the Dances with Films festival in 2010.

Hefner also worked as a writer, director, and editor on the feature film “Washed.”

From 2011 through 2016, the popular star kid worked as an editor at AOL Studious on digital and VR projects. He also worked with some affiliate sites at the time, including The List, Translogic, Hardwired, and Makers.

Hefner formerly worked as an editor at Oath from October 2016 to October 2017. He then resumed his independent career, working as a freelance editor for Netflix’s internal productions. He worked with Hay Day, a successful mobile video game, and Hearst Television.

According to a 1983 Washington Post article, Hefner was previously a photographer.
