Where was Earl Sweatshirt? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Thebe Neruda Kgositsile is better known by his stage moniker Earl Sweatshirt. He was born on February 24, 1994, in Chicago, Illinois. His mother, Cheryl Harris, was a law professor, and his father, Keorapetse Kgositsile, was a poet and political activist. When he was eight years old, his parents got divorced. His formative years were mostly spent in Los Angeles.

Earl completed his studies at Santa Monica Middle School and the UCLA Lab School. He was eventually transferred to the residential school “Coral Reef Academy” in Samoa due to a number of behavioral issues.

Earl Sweatshirt developed a career in rap thanks to his early passion in the genre. He started rapping as SlyTenedencies after completing boarding school. Later, he became a successful member of the Los Angeles-based rap group “Old Future.”

How did Earl Sweatshirt start his Professional Career?

When Earl was fifteen years old, he first started rapping. He started out as “SlyTendencies.” He began rapping in front of an audience in 2008 and previously used social media to share his unique recordings. His most well-liked hits in 2008 were “Mixtape” and “Kitchen Cutlery.” Later, he and three friends created the rap group “Backpackers.”

Tyler, a well-known American rapper, found Earl through his Myspace videos. Tyler then encouraged Earl to join his group, “Odd Future.” He quickly rose to fame thanks to his superb rapping skills, and he also had the chance to be featured in a few publications, including Fader, Spin, and Billboard.

In 2010, Earl finally released his debut album, titled “Earl.” It was a smash success and one of the top albums of the year.
