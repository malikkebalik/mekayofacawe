Euro 2024 qualifying fixtures: Full schedule, groups, play-offs, dates and kick-off times

 Posted on September 24, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1632 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The schedule is set for Euro 2024 qualifying, with 23 teams due to join hosts Germany at the finals.

A draw in Frankfurt split 53 teams into 10 groups for the qualifying campaign, which runs from March to November 2023.

Ten group winners and 10 runners-up will qualify for the final tournament, with the remaining three spots decided via the 2022-23 Nations League play-offs in March 2024.

What about the Euro 2024 play-offs?

Scotland face Spain and Erling Haaland’s Norway in qualifying — but could also advance through the play-offs. Photo: Getty Images

Twelve teams, still to be decided, will compete in three play-off ‘paths’ to reach the tournament proper.

The 12 contenders are nominally the group winners of Nations Leagues A, B and C, but if those sides reach their finals through Euro 2024 qualifying, they will be replaced by the next best-ranked team in their league, with spaces filtering down to League D if necessary.

Scotland finished top of Nations League group B1, meaning they will have the back-up of a play-off spot if they fail to finish in the top two of their Euro 2024 qualifying group.

Fellow group winners Israel, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Serbia, Georgia, Greece, Turkey and Kazakhstan, alongside Nations League finalists Netherlands, Croatia, Spain and Italy, also have play-off berths to fall back on if necessary.

The play-offs comprise single-leg knockout games, with semi-finals on Tuesday March 21 followed by three finals on Sunday March 26.

And when is Euro 2024 itself?

The month-long Euro 2024 finals will start on Friday June 14, with the final scheduled to take place at Berlin’s 70,000-capacity Olympiastadion on Sunday July 14.

The tournament will take place across 10 host cities: Berlin, Cologne, Dortmund, Dusseldorf, Frankfurt, Gelsenkirchen, Hamburg, Leipzig, Munich and Stuttgart.
