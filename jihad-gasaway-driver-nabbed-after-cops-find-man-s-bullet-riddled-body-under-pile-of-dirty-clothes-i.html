MONTEZUMA, OHIO: Jihad Gasaway, 23, was arrested on Tuesday, October 3 after police found a bullet-riddled body in his car which was stranded in a ditch on Interstate 80 in Iowa. Patrol officers had found Gasaway trying to restart his car.

After the initial investigation, Gasaway produced a handgun from his pocket, which led to the search of his vehicle, states the arrest report.

The body of Kemp Harriel, 26, with multiple gunshot wounds, was then discovered in the passenger seat under a layer of dirty clothes.

The Chevrolet Malibu which Gasaway was driving was reportedly broken down in a ditch at 6 am.

He then told the officers about the handgun, which led to the discovery of Harriel and Gasaway's arrest.

At the Poweskiek County Sheriff's Office, Gasaway told officers under questioning that he covered Harriel because he “thought he was very cold.”

“(I) kept him warm because we were both cold, I just waited for police,” he said, as per the arrest report.

“I didn't know (victim) was gone until I watched the police eyes and read his lips that's when I started crying in the car. He was alive and talking to me.”

However, officials noted that Gasaway “did not notify anyone of the male subject inside the vehicle”, “did not call 911 to request medical assistance,” and did not tell troopers about Harriel until they found the body.

Gasaway claimed that Harriet was alive when the car broke down when asked by officials and stated that the two men had "got into it."

Investigators later determined the shooting happened on the passenger side of the vehicle. A 9mm shell casing was found in the front floorboard and two bullets were inside the front passenger door.

According to the arrest report, it was discovered upon examination that the shell casings had been fired by the same gun found on Gasaway.

When the autopsy found that Harriel died as a result of two gunshot wounds fired at close range, his death was ruled as homicide.

The charges against Gasaway were changed from abuse of corpse to first-degree murder on October 5.
