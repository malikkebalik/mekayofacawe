Early Life And Career

Kelley Jakle auditioned for the American Idols. She attended the Loretto High School where she participated in school plays. She also began writing her songs and founded her high school a capella group.

Jakle took part in season one of The Sing-Off, a singing competition televised on NBC, as part of a group called “The SoCals.” in 2009. The SoCals were eliminated during the third episode. In 2010, Jakle again competed on The Sing-Off, this time as part of “The Backbeats” and finished the contest in third place

She started the University of Southern California, majoring in communications. At USC, Kelley was the lead vocalist being part of a music band called “By the Way.” In junior year, Jakle released her first EP named Spare Change. Also, Kelley’s band released their self-titled EP in 2007.

After graduating from USC, Jakle began her career as an actress in Los Angeles. Kelley’s first film role was “Jessica” in the 2012 musical comedy “Pitch Perfect.” Jakle appeared in 42, a biographical film about baseball player Jackie Robinson co-starring Harrison Ford as Branch Rickey, Jakle’s great-grandfather in 2013.

Jakle reprised her role as Jessica in “Pitch Perfect 2” in 2015 and “Pitch Perfect 3” in 2017. In 2013, Jakle released a cover of “Ain’t It Fun” by Paramore. Jakle originated the role of Marilyn Monroe on July 29, 2016.
