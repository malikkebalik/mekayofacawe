Overall Meaning

The song Chalk It Out by Lil Wayne and Brisco is a deeply introspective and contemplative piece that showcases Lil Wayne's lyrical skills while exploring themes such as self-reflection, being true to oneself, and overcoming obstacles. The chorus is a repeated mantra of "let it all work out," suggesting the need to simply let life take its course and trust that everything will fall into place eventually. The verses are deeply personal, with Lil Wayne reflecting on his own experiences and struggles.


In the first verse, Lil Wayne acknowledges that he's made money but insists that it won't change him or come between him and his true self. He's also sipping on something that keeps him energized; he later refers to it as a "shit that give a flower color." Lil Wayne also speaks about facing setbacks from people pretending to be cool with him but ultimately betraying him, along with how challenging the game has been. And then, he goes into "letting it all work out." The second verse sees Lil Wayne reflecting on his life, acknowledging how far he has come and all the things he has lost in the process. The verse is a tribute to his mother, who provided the gun that nearly took his life but is also an avatar for self-reflecting and pull oneself together.
