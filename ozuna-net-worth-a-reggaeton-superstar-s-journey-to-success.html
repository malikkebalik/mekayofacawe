Early Life and Career Beginnings

Born as Juan Carlos Ozuna Rosado on March 13, 1992, in San Juan, Puerto Rico, Ozuna faced economic hardships growing up and was raised by his grandmother after losing his father at a young age.

Inspired by his Christian faith and gifted with a microphone from his uncle, Ozuna realized his passion for music at the age of 12.

After performing at local venues and self-promoting music videos, Ozuna moved to New York City’s Washington Heights neighborhood in 2010.

However, facing similar challenges to his hometown, he returned to Puerto Rico after three years to pursue his musical aspirations.

Musical Style and Influences

Ozuna’s musical style primarily encompasses reggaeton and trap, with influences from salsa, bachata, and various Latin urban music strains.

Career Milestones

Ozuna’s breakthrough came with his debut studio album, “Odisea,” in 2017, featuring collaborations with renowned artists such as J Balvin and Nicky Jam.

The album’s massive success on the Top Latin Albums chart for 46 weeks catapulted Ozuna to global fame.

Subsequent albums like “Aura,” “Nibiru,” and “ENOC” continued to dominate the Latin charts, showcasing his consistency and talent as a performer and producer.

Notable collaborations with Cardi B, Selena Gomez, and DJ Snake on “Taki Taki” further expanded his international reach.

Personal Life and Philanthropy

Ozuna is a family man, married to Taina Marie Meléndez, with two children named Sofía and Jacob.

Apart from his music career, Ozuna has a passion for collecting watches and even owns the Puerto Rico National League basketball team Baloncesto Nacional Superior.

In 2017, Ozuna launched the charity “Odisea Children,” dedicated to supporting healthcare initiatives, showcasing his commitment to making a positive impact on society.
