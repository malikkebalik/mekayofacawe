Ajeé Wilson is widely recognized as one of the top female 800m runners in the world. Wilson is a middle-distance runner who competes for the United States. She is the defending global indoor champion, having won the title in March of last year in Belgrade 2022. She won the prized gold medal in the 800 metres with a time of 1:59.09, which was also her best performance of the season. Additionally, she had earlier won two silver medals at the World Indoor Championships in 2016 and 2018. She also won two bronze medals at the World Athletics Championships (outdoors).

She grew up in Neptune Township, New Jersey, in the United States. Furthermore, Wilson hails from a sporting family. Zachary Wilson, her father, used to play American football, soccer, and baseball. Her mother, Tonya Anderson, was able to split her time between high jump and 400 and 800m hurdles. Wilson quickly transitioned from being one of the best high school athletes in the nation to racing as a professional and joining the senior team. Wilson chose to stay near home and train alongside her coach Derek Thompson and the Juventus Track Club of Philadelphia after completing high school education from New Jersey’s Neptune Academy of Allied Health and Science in 2012.

At the World Junior Championships in 2012, she won the gold medal in the 800m. At the 2013 World Championships, Wilson ran the 800 meters in the quickest time among junior runners. In 2014, she won gold medals in the 800m at the USA Indoor Track and Field Championships as well as the USA Outdoor Track and Field Championships. She competed for the USA on the team that at the 2015 World Relay Championships broke the distance medley relay world record (alongside Treniere Moser, Sanya Richards-Ross and Shannon Rowbury). She claimed the 800m event at the USA Indoor Track and Field Championships in 2016. The 28-year-old competed for the United States in the 2016 Olympics in Rio de Janeiro but did not win a medal.
